## üé¨ PART 2: SET UP OBS STUDIO (15 minutes)

### Step 1: Configure OBS for Screen Recording

**Add Display Capture:**

1. In OBS, look at "Sources" panel (bottom)
2. Click the "+" button
3. Select "Display Capture"
4. Name it "Screen"
5. Click OK
6. Click OK again (default settings are fine)

**Add Audio Input:**

1. Click "+" in Sources again
2. Select "Audio Input Capture"
3. Name it "Microphone"
4. Select your microphone from dropdown
5. Click OK

**Test Your Setup:**

1. Click "Start Recording" (bottom right)
2. Say "Testing 1, 2, 3"
3. Click "Stop Recording"
4. Click "File" ‚Üí "Show Recordings"
5. Play the video - can you see screen and hear yourself?

**Adjust Settings (if needed):**

1. Click "Settings" (bottom right)
2. Go to "Output"
3. Set "Recording Quality" to "High Quality"
4. Go to "Video"
5. Set "Base Resolution" to 1920x1080
6. Set "FPS" to 30
7. Click "OK"

### Option A: Quick Edit in OBS (Recommended for Speed)

**If your recording is good:**

1. Watch it once
2. Note timestamps of mistakes

**Combine clips:**

1. Download Kdenlive (free) or use Windows Video Editor

3. Cut out mistakes
4. Add transitions (fade in/out)
5. Export as MP4

1. Go to canva.com
2. Click "Video"
3. Upload your OBS recording
4. Trim mistakes
5. Add text overlays for:
   - "73% of developers struggle..." (0:20)
   - "5 Major Integrations" (2:00)
   - "+35 Point Average Improvement" (2:20)
6. Add your end screen slide
7. Download as MP4

### Option C: Edit in DaVinci Resolve (Most Professional)

1. Download DaVinci Resolve (free)
2. Import your recording
3. Cut mistakes
4. Add text overlays
5. Add background music (low volume, 10%)
6. Color correct (make it pop!)
7. Export as MP4 (H.264, 1080p, 30fps)
